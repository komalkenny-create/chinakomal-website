ADNOC Awards Major Framework Agreement for Drilling Equipment Spares

June 24, 2026

ABU DHABI, UAE – ADNOC Drilling has awarded a significant framework agreement for the supply of drilling equipment spare parts and consumables covering its entire fleet of onshore and offshore rigs. The agreement, valued at an estimated USD 85 million over a five-year term, encompasses mud pump fluid end parts, blowout preventer seal assemblies, solids control screen decks, and drill string components for the operator's expanding rig fleet.

CHINA KOMAL International Technology Co., Ltd., as a qualified supplier under the framework, expects to deliver a substantial portion of the equipment spares covered by the agreement. The company has maintained an approved vendor status with ADNOC Drilling since 2023 following a comprehensive audit of its manufacturing facilities, quality management systems, and supply chain capabilities.

"This framework agreement represents one of the most significant procurement commitments in the Middle Eastern drilling sector this year," said a CHINA KOMAL executive. "ADNOC Drilling is operating one of the most active rig fleets in the world, and their spares demand profile aligns well with our manufacturing strengths—particularly in mud pump fluid ends, BOP seal kits, and solids control wear parts. We have already ramped up production capacity at our Beijing plant to support the anticipated order volume."

UAE Production Capacity Expansion Drives Demand

ADNOC's broader strategy to expand UAE crude oil production capacity to 5 million barrels per day by 2027 is driving increased drilling activity across the company's onshore and offshore concessions. ADNOC Drilling currently operates over 100 rigs and has announced plans to add a further 15 units by mid-2027, including six new-build offshore jackups and nine land rigs. Each new rig addition requires a fully stocked inventory of spare parts and consumables, creating sustained demand for fluid end components, shaker screens, and downhole tools.

The framework agreement consolidates ADNOC Drilling's previously fragmented spares procurement into a structured long-term arrangement with a select group of approved manufacturers. This consolidation is expected to reduce lead times, standardize part quality across the fleet, and lower total procurement costs through volume-based pricing.

Regional Warehousing and Logistics

To support the framework agreement, CHINA KOMAL has expanded its warehousing capacity at its Dubai Regional Logistics Center, adding 3,000 square meters of climate-controlled storage space dedicated to ADNOC-specific inventory. The expanded facility maintains buffer stocks of the most commonly ordered items, including mud pump liners and pistons for Gardner Denver PZ-11, PZ-9, and NOV Mission pumps; BOP seal kits for Cameron and NOV Shaffer stacks rated from 5,000 to 15,000 psi; and screen panels compatible with Brandt, Derrick, and SWECO shaker systems.

"Having inventory physically located in Dubai means we can deliver critical spares to ADNOC's rig sites within 48 to 72 hours, and emergency orders can be fulfilled within 24 hours," the executive added. "For a drilling operation where an hour of rig downtime can cost USD 100,000 or more, that speed of response is a significant value proposition."

Quality Assurance

All components supplied under the framework agreement are manufactured in accordance with API 7-1, API 16A, and ADNOC's own technical specifications. CHINA KOMAL's quality system maintains ISO 9001:2015 certification and the company's manufacturing facility recently passed a renewal audit with zero non-conformances. Each fluid end component is hydrostatically pressure-tested at 1.5 times working pressure, and all BOP seal kits are supplied with material traceability documentation per ASTM and NACE standards.
