Coiled Tubing Drilling Gains Momentum in Mature Field Revitalization

June 17, 2026

BEIJING, China – The global coiled tubing drilling (CTD) market is projected to reach USD 6.2 billion in 2026, according to the latest industry analysis from Spears & Associates, as operators increasingly turn to rigless intervention techniques to extend the economic life of mature fields. CTD technology enables drilling and well intervention operations without a conventional drilling rig, offering significant cost savings for sidetracking, lateral drilling, and through-tubing re-entry in aging wellbores.

"Mature field revitalization is the dominant theme in upstream oil and gas today, and coiled tubing drilling has become the tool of choice for cost-constrained operators," said a CHINA KOMAL drilling equipment specialist. "When you can mobilize a CTD unit for one-third the cost of a conventional workover rig and reach total depth in half the time, the economic case writes itself. We are seeing particularly strong uptake in the Permian Basin, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia."

CTD Bottom-Hole Assemblies Drive Performance Gains

Recent advances in CTD bottom-hole assembly (BHA) design have significantly expanded the operating envelope of coiled tubing drilling. Modern BHAs incorporate electric-line-conveyed orientation tools, real-time gamma ray and resistivity sensors, and positive displacement motors capable of operating in holes as small as 3-1/2 inches. CHINA KOMAL supplies a full range of CTD-compatible drill string components, including non-magnetic drill collars, stabilizers, and hydraulic disconnect subs engineered for the tighter clearances characteristic of through-tubing operations.

"One of the key challenges in CTD is weight-on-bit management," the specialist explained. "Conventional drill pipe provides weight through gravity, but coiled tubing rests against the wellbore wall along its entire length, creating significant friction. Our latest generation of axial oscillation tools reduces sliding friction by up to 60%, allowing deeper lateral reach and more effective weight transfer to the bit. These tools have been tested in extended-reach laterals exceeding 4,500 feet in a single run."

Pressure Control Equipment for Live-Well Operations

Because CTD is commonly performed on live wells without killing the well, pressure control equipment is a critical component of any CTD spread. CHINA KOMAL offers a complete suite of CTD pressure control equipment, including quad-ram stripper assemblies, annular blowout preventers rated to 10,000 psi, and compact hydraulic power units designed for the space-constrained rig-up configurations typical of platform and offshore CTD operations.

"The pressure control requirements for CTD are different from conventional drilling," noted the company's product manager for well control equipment. "You need stripper elements that can seal around the OD of the coiled tubing while allowing continuous pipe movement, and you need BOP rams that can shear 1.5-inch to 2.875-inch coiled tubing and seal the wellbore. Our quad-stripper system uses a tandem arrangement of dual stripper elements, with the outer element serving as a backup that can be energized hydraulically without tripping the BOP stack."

Regional Market Growth

The Middle East continues to be the fastest-growing CTD market, with Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Oman all ramping up coiled tubing drilling programs as part of their broader mature field redevelopment strategies. ADNOC has announced plans to deploy an additional eight CTD units across its onshore and offshore fields by year-end, while Saudi Aramco's CTD fleet is expected to grow by 15% in 2026.

CHINA KOMAL has established a dedicated CTD equipment inventory at its Dubai logistics hub to serve the Middle Eastern market, with stocked items including stripper elements, BOP seal kits, and BHA components ready for same-week delivery. The company also provides field service support through its regional technical team.
